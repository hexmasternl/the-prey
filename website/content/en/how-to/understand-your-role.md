---
title: "Understand Your Role Mid-Game"
description: "Understand your role mid-game in The Prey — what Hunters and Prey should focus on once the head start ends and the clock is running."
keywords: ["understand your role", "Hunter tips", "Prey tips", "in-game strategy", "The Prey how-to"]
---

<ol class="steps">
<li>When the game starts, your role screen appears — <strong>Prey</strong> or <strong>Hunter</strong>. Read it. It tells you what to do next.</li>
<li>If you are <strong>Prey</strong>: the head start countdown is on screen. Move immediately. Do not wait for hunters to appear — they start tracking as soon as the head start ends.</li>
<li>If you are <strong>Hunter</strong>: the head start countdown is on screen too. Use the time to study the playfield boundary and plan your approach.</li>
<li>Open the map tab when the Active Hunt begins. Your position is the center marker. Prey pins appear at their last broadcast location.</li>
<li>As <strong>Prey</strong>: the map shows Hunter positions so you know which direction to avoid. Keep moving. Pinned positions are not real-time — assume Hunters are closer than the map shows.</li>
<li>As <strong>Hunter</strong>: cross-reference Prey pin positions with the terrain. Prey on the edge of the playfield have limited escape routes. Cut them off.</li>
<li>Watch the timer in the top bar. The Final Stretch warning fires at 5 minutes remaining. Prey — stay hidden and wait it out. Hunters — close in fast.</li>
<li>When the game ends, results are shown automatically. Both Prey and Hunters see who survived.</li>
</ol>
