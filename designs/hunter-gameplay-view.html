Screen Design — Hunter Gameplay

HUNTER VIEW

The map is command. The hunter scans the playfield at a glance — green dot is you, red dots are them. Three numbers in the HUD: how long is left, how far the closest prey, and when their positions next update. Pan and zoom freely; tap re-center to snap back. Eyes on the field. Move fast.

Screen States

Three representative states a hunter encounters during a game. Map pan, prey count, and HUD values change; the core layout never does.

Component Annotations

Map & Polygon Layer
Map tiles fill the full screen bleed. The playfield polygon is an SVG overlay with a dashed stroke: --hunter-dim border and fill: rgba(255,47,31,.04) — a faint red wash instead of the prey view's green. The hunter's own position is always a green filled circle with a slow-expanding ring (you are always green). Active / Passive preys render as red flashing circles (1.1 s cycle). Tagged or Out preys render as static grey circles (#888, no animation) — eliminated, last known position only. The map scrolls with the tile layer; the SVG polygon is re-projected whenever the map view changes.
Status Bar
Floats over the map — gradient fade so it does not hard-crop the background. Corner brackets default to --hunter red — the hunter's identity color. The role pill cycles through three states: HUNTING (red, normal play), ENDGAME (caution yellow, final stage), ON TARGET (signal green, prey within close range). All four corner brackets (top + bottom) follow the pill color — the entire frame shifts at once to signal state change. Right side: role tag in Special Elite, always hunter red.
HUD Panel
A 2×2 grid of four cells: Game Time (top-left), Active Preys (top-right), Closest Prey (bottom-left), Penalty (bottom-right). Active Preys shows the count of prey participants whose state is Active or Passive — Tagged and Out preys are excluded. This counter decrements live on participant-status-changed SSE events. The Closest Prey cell considers only Active/Passive preys with known GPS positions. The Penalty cell is dim ("—") when no penalty is active; it switches to --caution amber when a penalty is in effect. Below the grid, a full-width Next Update strip counts down to the next game engine broadcast: the moment prey dots may move. The bar and countdown adopt the same color as the Closest Prey cell, tying urgency to proximity.

Closest Prey Distance — Color Thresholds

The hunter's distance color scale is the inverse of the prey's distance scale. Where prey see green as safe (hunter far away), the hunter sees green as winning (prey close). Three discrete states; no interpolation — the abrupt switch reads faster in motion.

Next Prey Update Strip

A full-width strip below the 2-cell grid, counterpart to the prey view's Location Ping strip. It counts down to the next game engine broadcast — the moment prey positions are refreshed and the red dots on the map may move. The bar fills left-to-right as the next broadcast approaches; the countdown resets each cycle (30 s default, shorter in the final stage).

The bar and countdown adopt the current Closest Prey distance color: dim red when prey is far, caution yellow when approaching, signal green when in range. When a prey has an active penalty and is being tracked every cycle, the label changes to PENALTY — TRACKED and the bar animates in green at the accelerated 30 s rate.

Hunter Head-Start — Standby State

When the owner starts the game, preys get a head-start period during which the hunter may not move. This produces a distinct pre-hunt screen state not shown in the three main mockups above:

Standby HUD
The Closest Prey cell is replaced by a single large countdown — time until the hunter is released. The label reads "Move In" and the value counts down in seconds. The status pill shows STANDBY in ghost dim text. The Next Update strip is hidden (there are no prey positions to broadcast yet).
Map During Standby
The playfield polygon is visible. The hunter's green dot is centered on the map. No prey dots are shown — prey positions are not broadcast during the head-start period. The map is fully pannable and zoomable so the hunter can study the playfield before the hunt begins.
Release Transition
When the standby countdown hits zero, the pill snaps from STANDBY to HUNTING (red). Corner brackets switch from ghost to hunter red. The Closest Prey cell replaces the countdown. The Next Update strip appears. No full-screen modal interrupts the transition — the HUD simply repopulates in place.

Layout Rules

Map Centering & Re-center FAB
On load the map is centered on the hunter's GPS position — the green dot is at the viewport center. The hunter can freely pan and pinch-zoom to survey the playfield. When the user pans away from their position, a re-center FAB (⊕, 36 × 36 px, hunter red border) appears in the lower-right corner of the visible map area, above the HUD gradient. Tapping it snaps the map back to the hunter's current GPS position with a short ease animation and dismisses the FAB. The green dot always reflects the true GPS position regardless of map pan state.
Prey Dot States
Prey dots have two visual modes. Active / Passive preys render as red flashing dots (the existing 1.1 s flash cycle) — they are still in the game. Tagged or Out preys render as a static grey dot (#888, no animation, 60 % opacity) — they are eliminated and cannot rejoin. The grey dot remains visible so the hunter can see where the eliminated prey last broadcast, but it does not count toward "Closest Prey" or "Active Preys." The state switch happens immediately on receipt of a participant-status-changed SSE event — no poll required.
Touch Targets
The HUD grid is a pure readout — no tap targets within the four cells. Two actionable controls exist: the Re-center FAB (⊕, 36 × 36 px, above the HUD gradient) and the Tag Player button, which appears as a full-width action row below the Next Update strip. Tapping Tag Player opens a modal listing only Active/Passive preys; the hunter must confirm before the tag request is sent. The button is disabled while a tag request is in flight to prevent double-submission. Both controls must be at least 44 × 44 px actual tap target size.
