Screen Design — Prey Gameplay

PREY VIEW

The map is primary. Everything else recedes into a thin dark panel at the bottom edge. A prey glances down, reads three numbers in under a second, and keeps running. This screen never scrolls, never navigates. It just watches.

Component Annotations

Map & Polygon Layer
The map tile component fills the full screen bleed — no border, no margin. The playfield polygon is rendered as an SVG overlay with a dashed stroke: --signal-dim border and fill: rgba(100,255,0,.04) — barely visible, just enough to mark territory without obscuring the street layer below. The prey's own position is a green filled circle with an expanding ring pulse. The hunter's last known position is a red filled circle that flashes at 1.1 s intervals to signal "stale data, not live."
Status Bar
Floats over the map — gradient fade so it doesn't hard-crop the map behind it. Left: a pulsing role pill (HIDDEN green, SPOTTED caution, TAGGED ghost). Right: role tag in Special Elite. Corner brackets use --signal normally; switch to --hunter when the hunter is within danger range. This change propagates to all four corners (top + bottom) so the entire frame signals threat.
HUD Panel
Two cells (2×1 grid) for the two critical readouts, then a full-width ping strip below. Background is near-opaque void with a gradient fade to transparent at the top so it blends into the map seamlessly. All values use Special Elite at 26 px for the main number; sub-labels in 10 px uppercase PT Mono.

Game Timer — Color & Animation States

The timer is the second-most-important readout. Its color change is the earliest warning of the final stage.

Final stage (≤ 5 min left)

--hunter red + 500 ms flash loop. Corner brackets also switch to red.

The flash is a 500 ms opacity cycle from 100 % → 20 % → 100 % — fast enough to feel urgent, slow enough not to be epileptic. Honor prefers-reduced-motion: freeze to a static red value when the OS requests it.

Location Ping Strip

A full-width strip below the 2-cell grid. A countdown in Special Elite (MM:SS) counts down from the interval duration to zero, then resets. A thin progress bar fills left-to-right as time within the interval elapses. Both the bar and the countdown adopt the current threat color when the hunter is in caution or danger range — visually tying ping frequency to threat level.

During a penalty (10 s forced interval), the strip label changes to PENALTY PING and the bar fills red at an accelerated pace.

Layout Rules

Map Coverage
Map tiles fill 100 % of the viewport — no safe-area insets, no padding. Both the status bar and HUD panel float as positioned overlays with gradient fades, so the map bleeds behind them. The effect: the phone disappears and the playfield becomes the game.
HUD Height
The HUD panel is fixed height — approximately 120 px on a standard 390 px wide screen. It does not expand or shrink. The map content area is the full screen minus ~120 px at the bottom and ~50 px at the top for the status bar gradient. This keeps the viewable map area at roughly 75 % of the screen.
Touch Targets
The prey view is read-only during play. There are no tap targets in the HUD itself — it is a pure readout. A long-press on the screen MAY surface a context menu (e.g. report issue, end game) in a future iteration, but accidental taps must not trigger navigation.
