Section 00 — Foundations

The Concept

The Prey lives on a phone, but it's played in the street. The interface is not a destination — it's a tactical readout glanced at while running, hiding, and scanning rooftops. Every design decision flows from one idea: this is a surveillance instrument for a manhunt.

The visual language borrows from night-vision optics, GPS trackers, and field-radio comms: phosphor green burning against dead grey, typewriter stencils, monospace telemetry, and targeting reticles. It should feel covert, urgent, and a little dangerous.

Design Principles

01
Glanceable Over Beautiful
A player reads the screen at a sprint. Critical state — your role, the nearest threat, time left — must register in under a second. Hierarchy beats decoration.
02
Green Is Information, Not Wallpaper
The signal green is the most precious pixel on screen. Reserve it for live, actionable data. If everything glows, nothing does.
03
Two Sides, One System
Prey and Hunter share the same instrument. Role is communicated through color and a single accent — never two different apps.
04
The World Is The Canvas
The map and radar are primary. Chrome is thin, dark, and recedes so the playfield dominates.

Typography

Two faces, two jobs. Special Elite — a battered typewriter face — stamps every heading and key readout with the texture of a printed dossier. PT Mono handles all running text and data; its even rhythm reads like telemetry and keeps numbers aligned.

Rules

· Headings & numeric readouts in Special Elite. · Everything else in PT Mono. · Labels are UPPERCASE PT Mono with letter-spacing: 2px. · Special Elite has no bold weight — create emphasis with size and color, never faux-bold. · Keep line length under ~68 characters for body copy.

Section 04 — System

Buttons

Primary actions wear solid signal green with a glow on press — the single brightest element on screen. Hunter-side destructive actions (declare, tag, eliminate) use threat red. All button labels are Special Elite, uppercase.

VARIANTStap states

· Primary — one per screen, the main objective. · Ghost — secondary, outlined. · Hunter — irreversible / hostile actions only. · Disabled — line border, ghost text, no glow.

Section 09 — System

Motion

Motion mimics live instruments: a steady radar sweep, a pulse on every new ping, a brief glow-flare when a primary action fires. Avoid bouncy, playful easing — everything is mechanical and purposeful.

ACCESSIBILITY // Honor reduced-motion: freeze the radar sweep and replace pulses with a static halo when the OS requests it.

Section 10 — System

Voice & Tone

The app speaks like field comms: clipped, present-tense, second-person, no fluff. Imperatives over explanations. It addresses the player as an operative, never as a "user."

DO

"You are hidden. Keep moving."
"Hunter nearby. Break line of sight."
"Signal lost. Find open sky."
"Hunt begins in 00:10."

DON'T

"Oops! We couldn't find you 😅"
"You might want to consider moving."
"Error 403: location unavailable."
"Welcome back, valued player!"
